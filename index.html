Introduction to JEDI

What is the JEDI Project?

JEDI (Joint Exploration & Discovery Initiative) is New Zealands next leap in space exploration. Led by Global Robotics and in partnership with Rocket Lab and the New Zealand Space Agency (NZSA), the project will launch an autonomous satellite into Low Earth Orbit (LEO) to unlock new discoveries in lunar and planetary science.

Why is this project important?

  • Positions New Zealand as a global leader in space research.
  • Speeds up missions and lowers costs through innovative partnerships.
  • Provides open access to mission data for researchers worldwide.
  • Uses AI powered autonomy to collect high-value data continuously.

How will it work?

  • Global Robotics Designs, builds, and operates the satellite.
  • NZSA Handles regulatory approvals and ensures mission compliance.
  • Rocket Lab Launches the satellite and provides ground support from Mahia Peninsula.

After launch, the satellite will validate its systems and begin autonomous scientific operations, delivering insights that advance planetary research and inspire global collaboration.

When will it happen?

The JEDI mission follows a clearly defined schedule to ensure timely delivery and operation readiness:

  • Launch Date: The official launch date will be six months from the date of this proposals acceptance.
  • Transport & Final Integration: Rocket lab will arrange transportation of the satellite to the launch site and final checks will occur six weeks prior to launch.
  • Orbital Testing Phase: Immediately post launch, the satellite will deploy its solar panels and undergo orbit insertion validation, instrument calibration, and communications testing.
  • Lunar Transfer Phase: The transition to lunar operations will begin upon successful completion of the orbital testing and system performance phases.

JEDI Partners

Global Robotics
Leads mission design, hardware and software integration, and manages autonomous operations from Earth to lunar orbit.
Rocket Lab NZ
Provides funding support, policy direction, and ensures regulatory compliance throughout the mission lifecycle.
New Zealand Space Agency
Delivers launch services, including payload integration and orbital deployment using the Electron launch vehicle.

Objectives

Core Mission & Technical Execution

  • Successfully deploy, activate, and monitor the autonomous modular satellite throughout an extended-duration mission.
  • Collect, process, and transmit high-value space and planetary data using the AI-driven scientific instrument suite.
  • Validate the performance of propellant-less propulsion technology in a real space environment, proving its efficiency for long-duration, low-maintenance space exploration.
  • Demonstrate full systems integration across embedded systems, flight control software, ground telemetry stations, and mission planning environments in real mission conditions.

Scientific Discovery & Data Utilization

  • Achieve continuous, autonomous scientific observations of lunar and planetary environments, generating a comprehensive dataset that advances our understanding of these celestial bodies.
  • Utilize AI algorithms to identify and prioritize high-value scientific targets in real-time, optimizing data collection efficiency and relevance.
  • Facilitate open access to mission data for the global scientific community, promoting collaborative research and maximizing the mission's scientific impact.
  • Publish findings in peer-reviewed journals and present at international conferences, contributing to the broader body of knowledge in planetary science and autonomous space exploration.

Partnerships & Capacity Building

  • Establish and maintain strong partnerships with key stakeholders, including Rocket Lab NZ and the New Zealand Space Agency, ensuring alignment of mission goals and collaborative success.
  • Develop local expertise in autonomous space systems through targeted training programs and knowledge transfer initiatives, enhancing New Zealand's capabilities in space technology.
  • Engage with educational institutions to inspire the next generation of scientists and engineers, fostering interest in STEM fields through outreach programs and mission involvement.
  • Document lessons learned and best practices from the mission to inform future projects and contribute to the global discourse on autonomous space exploration.
