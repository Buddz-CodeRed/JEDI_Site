About Us

At Global Robotics, we are driven by a vision to push the boundaries of what is possible in space exploration. Based in the Bay of Plenty, New Zealand, we specialize in creating autonomous systems, advanced robotics, and intelligent onboard technologies that can thrive in the extreme environments beyond Earth. Our exprtise spans modular spacecraft design, self-directed robotic systems, space-grade material engineering, and AI-powered data processing. From thermal and radiation protection to the development of cutting-edge scientific instruments, every solution we create is designed to support long-duration missions and deliver meaningful discoveries.

Currently, our dedicated research and integration labs in New Zealand serve as the hub for innovation, where a multicultural team of aerospace engineers, software developers, and robotics specialists collaborate on projects ranging from CubeSat development to search-and-rescue satellites in Low Earth Orbit. Through academic and commercial partnerships, we continue to pioneer technologies that not only serve scientific exploration but also provide real-world benefits here on Earth.

Our satellites are built on a modular, autonomous architecture — optimized for durability, adaptability, and functionality in the harshest environments. This flexibility allows us to tailor missions to specific goals, whether in orbit, on the lunar surface, or in deep space.

Our upcoming satellite deployment aboard Rocket Lab’s Electron launch vehicle marks the next step in this journey—validating our autonomous platform in orbit and laying the foundation for scalable, interplanetary missions. At Global Robotics, we are committed to shaping the future of exploration through reliability, intelligence, and innovation.

Our Expertise

  • Modular Spacecraft Design


    We build scalable, modular spacecrafts subsystems that allow for adaptation to specific mission goals whether orbiting, landing, or sample return missions.

  • Autonomous Robotic Systems


    Our robotic platforms are engineered for planetary and orbital missions, featuring intelligent navigation, fault-tolerant systems, and self-directed AI that enables true autonomy beyond Earth.

  • Space-Grade Material Engineering


    We create customized thermal shielding and radiation resistant hardware to protect electronic components and systems during prolonged exposure to space.

  • AI-Powered Data Processing


    Every spacecraft built by us uses machine learning to interpret data in real-time, prioritize transmissions, and autonomously respond to new environment conditions or hazards.

  • Cutting-Edge Scientific Instruments


    Our engineering teams develop both light and heavy weight, durable scientific instruments for use in the harsh environment of space.
