Why It Works
  • New Zealand-based innovation: Designed, developed, and tested by New Zealanders within New Zealand, show casing the nation’s growing expertise and reputation in advanced space systems.
  • Cost-effective mission architecture: Smart engineering and resource-efficient systems ensure high performance with a lean budget, making space science more accessible and sustainable.
  • STEM and educational outreach potential: Offers opportunities for academic collaboration and national inspiration through accessible mission data and involvement in a groundbreaking local space project.
  • Propellant-less propulsion system: Demonstrates cutting-edge, low-maintenance space mobility without traditional fuel systems.
  • Autonomous navigation and recovery protocol: Operates with minimal human intervention, featuring self-correcting algorithms for navigation, fault recovery, and end-of-life deorbit capability.
  • Modular, scalable satellite design: Built with flexibility and autonomy, enabling adaptable deployment and real-time decision-making in space.
  • Multi-spectrum scientific instrumentation: Advance sensor suite capable of collection highvalue data across multiple wavelengths, tailored for planetary research, anomaly detection, and AI-based onboard analysis.
Where We Grow

Our core mission focuses on radical technological advancement and pushing the boundaries of solar system exploration. We are committed to developing next-generation missions explicitly targeting the outer solar system and deeper space, leveraging our modular satellite platforms for adaptable and long-duration journeys. Key to this is pioneering commercial satellite servicing and repair technologies to ensure the longevity of our assets. Furthermore, the robust multi-spectrum data collected by our missions will be deployed to improve global climate and environment monitoring, creating high-value data streams. These proven, autonomous technologies and platforms will form the foundation of our export strategy, making advanced space systems accessible to global markets.

Our strategic roadmap is centered on establishing global commercial influence and delivering significant national benefits. We aim to attract international research partnerships to collaborate on advanced space missions, thereby expanding New Zealand’s role as a key contributor in space science. This success will directly benefit the domestic economy by creating educational programs and commercial spin-offs that leverage mission technologies and data for new industries. Finally, by developing expertise in cutting-edge, low-maintenance space systems, we are also strategically building the foundations to support the future space tourism sector and pioneer new forms of commercial operation.

How We Earn

Explore diverse approaches to generate income in the space industry, from data licensing to collaborative mission funding, maximizing commercial potential.

Sell high-resolution imagery and scientific datasets to universities, research agencies, and enterprises.

Offer hosted payload slots for clients to test hardware or sensors in space environments.

Provide mission-ready platforms for clients who require space access without owning full infrastructure.

License internally developed onboard systems, AI modules, and sensor tech to other space companies.

Monetize STEM programs and educational access through licensing of real mission data and resources.

Leverage government or academic grants to partially fund mission development while retaining tech ownership and commercialization rights.

Offer mission planning, integration services, and space systems development consultancy to third parties.
